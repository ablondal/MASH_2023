Bowling Balls

Bob has a special research project. He's trying to analyze how bowling balls bounce and collide. Recently, he set up a whole bunch of bowling balls in a large room and started them rolling.

At first, Bob was watching and noting all the collisions by hand. Each collision made a big loud satisfying TOC. But the balls just kept on rolling and colliding and TOCing, and Bob got more and more tired. Finally, Bob had enough, and set up an automated system.

He put a sensor in the center of each bowling ball, so he could track exactly where each ball was at all times. Furthermore, he hooked up a microphone to his computer. Every time it heard a TOC, it stored the locations of all the bowling balls.

Satisfied, Bob went to bed and left his experiment running overnight. When he woke up, he could still hear the TOC TOC TOC of the bowling balls. He rushed downstairs to see the balls still rolling and colliding! Bob was horrified, because he knew this meant he had generated way too much data to analyze by hand. He has asked for your help.

Given the radius of a bowling ball, and the positions of all the balls, recorded as a TOC was heard, can you identify which balls are the ones currently colliding?

Input

The first line of input will consist of two integers $r$ and $n$, denoting the radius of all the bowling balls, and the number of bowling balls.

The next $n$ lines will each have two real numbers, $x_i$ and $y_i$ for $1 \leq i \leq n$, denoting the coordinates of the $i$th bowling ball at the instant of the collision.

It is guaranteed that at most two bowling balls are colliding. It is guaranteed that the coordinates are accurate to an absolute or relative value of $10^-6$.

$0 \leq r, n, |x_i|, |y_i| \leq 10^5$

Output

If bowling balls $i$ and $j$ are colliding, where $i < j$, print $i$ and $j$ on a line, space-seperated.

Sample Input 1

1 2
0 0
1 0

Sample Output 1

1 2

There are only two balls, and they are colliding!

Sample Input 2

5 4
0 0
10 -8
6 8
20 0

Sample Output 2

1 3

Sample Input 3

5 4
1.1 1.1
5.2460825 -8
7.9 8.9
12.9 0

Sample Output 3

1 2
