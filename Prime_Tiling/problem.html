Prime Tiling

After saving for 30 years, Bob was finally able to buy a new house in the Vancouver area, and now wants to re-tile the floor. The area that Bob wants to re-tile is rectangle shaped, $N$ metres by $M$ metres. Unfortunately, the only tiles that Bob can buy from the store are rectangular tiles with dimensions of $1 \times P$ metres, where $P$ is a prime number. Bob loves a neat floor and wants all of the tiles he uses to have the same size. He also does not want to cut any tiles. For a given $N$, $M$ and $P$, help Bob determine if it is possible to tile his $N \times M$ floor with $1 \times P$ tiles such that there are no overlapping tiles and the floor is completely covered.

Input

A single line containing three integers $N$, $M$ and $P$.

$1 \leq N, M \leq 1000$
$2 \leq P \leq 100$, $P$ is guaranteed to be a prime number.

Output

YES if it is possible for Bob to tile his $N \times M$ floor using only tiles of dimension $1 \times P$.
NO if it is not possible.

Sample Input 1

6 2 3

Sample Output 1

YES

Sample Input 2

4 7 5

Sample Output 2

NO
