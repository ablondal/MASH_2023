Climbing Wall

Bob is starting to learn how to climb! Right now, he's at a climbing gym, considering the wall in front of him.

He climbs by occupying a single handhold, and moving to one within 5 feet of his current handhold, repeating over and over. His goal is to touch the top of the wall, which he can do from a handhold within 5 feet of it. He starts at the bottom of the wall, and can reach up to 5 feet up to the first handhold. Furthermore, Bob is an impatient climber, and will only move to a handhold strictly higher on the wall than his current one.

However, Bob is also savvy, and wants to find the easiest way up the wall. He's assigned a score between 0 and 9 to each handhold, its difficulty. Help him find the path that minimizes the sum of the difficulty of handholds!

Input

The first line will consist of two integers $h$ and $w$, the height and width of the wall in feet.
The next $h+2$ lines each consist of $w+2$ characters describing the wall. Each character consists of a 1 square foot area.
A number indicates a handhold of that difficulty.
A dot indicates an area without a handhold.
Please look at the sample outputs for examples.

$1 \leq h \leq 100$
$1 \leq w \leq 100$

Output

Output the minimum sum of handhold difficulties that Bob must take to reach the top of the wall.
If it is impossible to reach the top of the wall, output -1.

Sample Input 1

8 11
+-----------+
|...........|
|...........|
|.......3...|
|...4.......|
|...........|
|......2....|
|...........|
|...........|
+-----------+

Sample Output 1

4

Bob can reach the handholds of difficulty 2 and 4 from the bottom. From the handhold 4, he can reach the top of the climbing wall, but he cannot from the handhold 2.
Thus his best path is to move to handhold 4, then reach the top of the wall.
