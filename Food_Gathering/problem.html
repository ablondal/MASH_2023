Food Gathering

Today, Bob and Alice are ants.

Alice is the queen, and her colony consists of $n$ chambers and $n-1$ tunnels between the chambers. Her colony is special: there is exactly one way to use tunnels to get between any two chambers in the colony.

Workers have been busy all day collecting food and putting it into $k$ various chambers around the colony. Alice has now assigned Bob a job: collect all the food spread around the colony and bring it to Alice.

Alice and Bob both start in chamber 1, the royal chamber. Bob is very strong and can carry as much food as needs to. Calculate the minimum distance Bob will have to walk to gather all the food and bring it back to Alice in chamber 1.

Input

The first line of input will consist of integers $n$ and $k$, the number of chambers in the colony, and the number of chambers with food.

Following are $n-1$ lines with integers $a_i, b_i, d_i$, indicating that there is a tunnel between chambers $a_i$ and $b_i$ with length $d_i$.

Finally, there is a line with $k$ integers $f_1, f_2, \dots, f_k$. These indicate the chambers with food in them.

$1 \leq k \leq n \leq 1000$

$1 \leq a_i, b_i, f_i \leq n$

$1 \leq d_i \leq 10^5$

Output

Output the minimum distance Bob will have to walk to gather all the food and bring it to Alice.

Sample Input 1

4 2
1 2 1
2 3 1
1 4 1
4 3

Sample Output 1

6

One way for Bob to retrieve the food optimally is to walk to chamber $2$, then $3$, pick up the food there, return to $2$ then $1$, then walk to chamber $4$ and pick up that food, then return to $1$ and drop it off.

Sample Input 2

3 1
1 2 1
1 3 1
1

Sample Output 2

0

The only food is already in Alice's chamber!

Sample Input 3

6 2
1 6 9
6 4 9
3 2 3
6 3 6
6 5 8
4 6

Sample Output 3

36
