Stacked Deck

Alice and Bob are playing a game with a custom deck of cards. The deck contains $N$ cards, each with a single number on it.

The game works as follows: Alice and Bob are each dealt $K$ cards. In a single round, both players choose one card from their hand and play it simultaneously. The person who played the card with the higher number gets $(a-b)^2$ points, where $a$ is the number on the higher card and $b$ is the number on the lower card. If both players play a card with the same number, nobody gets points in that round. Once a card is played, it can not be used again. After $K$ rounds, both players run out of cards and the person with the higher number of points wins.

Bob is tired of losing, so he decided to stack the deck so that he gets the $K$ highest cards and Alice gets the $K$ lowest cards. Despite stacking the deck, Bob doesnâ€™t know what order Alice will play her cards. How many points is Bob guaranteed to win by no matter how Alice plays her cards?

Input

The first line contains two integers $N$ and $K$, the number of cards in the deck and the number of cards dealt to each player respectively.

The next line contains $N$ integers, where the $a_i$ is the number on the $i$th card in the deck.

$1 \leq N \leq 1e5$
$1 \leq K \leq N/2$
$1 \leq a_i \leq 1e5

Output

The number of points that Bob is guarunteed to win by after stacking the deck, regardless of the order that Alice plays her cards.

Sample Input 1

3 1
4 2 5

Sample Output 1

9

Bob chooses to stack the deck such that he gets the card with $5$, and Alice gets the card with $2$.
During round 1, Bob plays $5$ and Alice plays $2$ (they only have one option for the card to play). Bob gets $(5-2)^2 = 9$ points.
After the game ends, Bob has 9 points and Alice has 0 points.

Sample Input 2

6 2
3 1 5 6 3 5

Sample Output 2

25

After stacking the deck, Bob is dealt cards $5$, $6$ and Alice is dealt cards $1$, $3$
There are four possibilities for the order that cards are played:

 Possibility 1:
  Round 1: Alice plays $1$, Bob plays $5$.
  Round 2: Alice plays $3$, Bob plays $6$.
  Bob wins by 25 points.

 Possibility 2:
  Round 1: Alice plays $1$, Bob plays $6$.
  Round 2: Alice plays $3$, Bob plays $5$.
  Bob wins by 29 points.

 Possibility 3:
  Round 1: Alice plays $3$, Bob plays $5$.
  Round 2: Alice plays $1$, Bob plays $6$.
  Bob wins by 29 points.

 Possibility 4:
  Round 1: Alice plays $3$, Bob plays $6$.
  Round 2: Alice plays $1$, Bob plays $5$.
  Bob wins by 25 points.

Therefore, Bob is guaranteed to win by at least 25 points no matter the order that the cards are played.
