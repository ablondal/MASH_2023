City Planning

Alice just started her new job as city planner.

She is super fond of walkable cities, and wants to make her city as walkable as possible. However, she knows that she can't just eliminate cars overnight, as many businesses would be quite unhappy about that. Instead, she's decided to make all of the streets in the city one-way streets. This way, she can make the streets narrower, add a bike lane everywhere, and widen the sidewalk!

The major snag in this plan is that after her changes, there should still be at least one way to get from anywhere in the city to anywhere else by car. Alice is looking hard at the city, but needs your help in figuring out if her plan is even possible!

Input

The first line of input will consist of two integers $N$ and $M$, the number of locations in the city, and the number of streets in the city, respectively.

The next $M$ lines will consist each of two integers $a$ and $b$, indicating that there is a street between locations $a$ and $b$. It is guaranteed that there is currently at least one way to get from every location to every other location. There is at most one street connecting any two locations.

$1 \leq N \leq 100$, $N-1 \leq M \leq N(N-1)/2$
$1 \leq a, b \leq N$

Output

If there exists a way of turning all streets into one-way streets such that every location can be reached from every location, print YES.

Otherwise, print NO.

Sample Input 1

2 1
1 2

Sample Output 1

NO

If the street were directed from 1 to 2, there would be no way from 2 to 1. Likewise if the street were directed from 2 to 1.

Sample Input 2

3 3
3 1
2 1
3 2

Sample Output 2

YES

The streets can be directed 3 -> 2 -> 1 -> 3.
