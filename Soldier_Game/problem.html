Soldier Game

Alice and Bob are playing a game. They have a $1 \times 32$ board in front of them, with toy soldiers on some of the squares.

The rules of the game are simple: Players take turns removing one of the soldiers. The key is that a soldier can only be removed if there is a soldier in one of its neighbouring squares.

If a player cannot make any moves, they lose. Bob goes first. Assuming that both players play optimally, who wins?

Input

The input will consist of a string of 32 characters, representing the board.

A ! represents a square with a soldier, and a . is a square without a soldier.

Output

Bob if Bob wins.
Alice if Alice wins.

Sample Input 1

............!!!.................

Sample Output 1

Bob

Bob can remove the middle soldier. Then, the soldiers left each stand alone, so Alice has no moves and loses.

Sample Input 2

............!!...!!.............

Sample Output 2

Alice

No matter whether Bob removes a soldier from the first or second group, Alice can then remove one from the other group, and Bob will have no moves and lose.

Sample Input 3

...........!!!!..!!!............

Sample Output 3

Bob

Sample Input 4

!!..!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!.!.!!!.!!!!

Sample Output 4

Alice
